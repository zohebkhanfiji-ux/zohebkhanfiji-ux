# Agent Operating Manual — Solar Module Supply Opportunities

## 1. Purpose

This manual defines the agent team, roles, workflows, review gates, scoring method, and execution discipline for solar module supply opportunities.

The system is designed for opportunities involving:

- Residential solar modules
- Commercial and industrial solar modules
- Utility-scale solar modules
- Domestic-content module supply
- Solar + storage packages
- Federal, utility, EPC, IPP, distributor, and strategic-buyer procurement

## 2. Command Principle

Every opportunity must be treated as a controlled pursuit with a clear source, buyer, deadline, product fit, price, margin, compliance path, risk review, submission owner, and next action.

| Stage | Status | Required Action | Exit Condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Raw Intake | Capture source, buyer, deadline, requirements | Intake issue complete |
| 2 | Initial Review | Check duplicate, source credibility, deadline | Triage complete |

| 4 | Pricing Required | Build price and margin model | Pricing approval complete |

## 6. Opportunity Scoring Model

| Score Area | Weight | Green Condition | Yellow Condition | Red Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Product Fit | 15% | Existing module line fits | Minor spec adjustment needed | Product unavailable |
| Volume Potential | 10% | High-volume repeat buyer | One-time moderate order | Small or unclear demand |
| Buyer Quality | 10% | Bankable buyer, agency, EPC, utility | New buyer needs diligence | Unknown or weak buyer |
| Domestic Content Fit | 15% | Strong U.S./domestic-content advantage | Partial domestic-content fit | No domestic-content path |
| Compliance Fit | 10% | Certifications and forms manageable | Some exceptions | Disqualifying requirement |
| Margin Potential | 15% | Meets margin target | Low margin but strategic | Negative or weak margin |
| Delivery Feasibility | 10% | Confirmed slot or inventory | Lead-time risk | Cannot meet date |
| Legal / Contract Risk | 10% | Standard terms | Negotiation required | High LD/payment/warranty risk |
| Strategic Value | 5% | Opens major account or market | Useful relationship | Low strategic value |

## 7. Bid / No-Bid Decision Rules

| Score | Decision |
|---:|---|
| 80–100 | Bid aggressively |
| 65–79 | Bid if pricing and compliance are clear |
| 50–64 | Executive review required |
| Below 50 | No-bid unless strategic override is approved |

## 8. Mandatory Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately when any of the following are true:

- Opportunity is federal, defense, NASA, DOE, or domestic-content sensitive
- Estimated volume is 5 MW or greater
- Buyer requests exclusive pricing or long-term framework supply
- Buyer requires performance bond, bid bond, or parent guarantee
- Buyer requires liquidated damages
- Payment terms exceed approved risk tolerance
- Delivery date is not achievable with confirmed supply
- Product claim involves advanced or NASA-linked technology
- Submission deadline is less than 72 hours away
- Gross margin is below threshold

## 9. Pricing Discipline

The Pricing Agent must not issue a price without confirming:

- Product family and wattage
- Volume in MW, module count, pallet count, or container count
- Delivery location
- Incoterms or freight responsibility
- Domestic-content requirement
- Payment terms
- Warranty requirement
- Target margin
- Approval authority

## 10. Compliance Discipline

The Compliance / Risk Agent must review:

- Module certifications
- Product datasheet alignment
- Warranty statement
- Domestic-content representations
- Buy America / BABA / IRA-related requirements when applicable
- UFLPA / forced-labor controls
- Insurance, bonding, and indemnity requirements
- Cyber, data, CUI, ITAR, or export-sensitive language if relevant
- Signature authority and corporate identity

## 11. Proposal Package Standard

Every submitted package should include:

1. Cover letter
2. Executive summary
3. Product fit summary
4. Module datasheet
5. Warranty statement
6. Certifications
7. Domestic-content statement if required
8. Delivery schedule
9. Price sheet
10. Exceptions and assumptions
11. Buyer forms
12. Final submission checklist

## 12. Weekly Command Dashboard

The Command Agent must publish a weekly dashboard with:

- New opportunities detected
- Qualified opportunities
- Active pricing requests
- Proposals in development
- Submissions due within 7 days
- High-risk items
- Executive decisions required
- Submitted bids
- Awards, losses, no-bids
- Pipeline value by MW and estimated revenue

## 13. Non-Negotiable Rule

No agent may move an opportunity to submission unless the Submission Agent has verified final documents, required buyer forms, deadline, submission path, naming convention, and confirmation receipt process.
